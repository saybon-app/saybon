What DELF is

DELF is an internationally recognized French language diploma awarded by the French Ministry of Education. It evaluates practical communication in French across four key skills: listening, reading, writing, and speaking.

The 4 Skills
Listening You practise understanding announcements, conversations, instructions, and spoken information.
Reading You learn to understand notices, messages, short texts, articles, and exam-style written tasks.
Writing You prepare to write clearly for the level: forms, messages, emails, opinions, and structured responses.
Speaking You build confidence for oral tasks, role plays, personal questions, and guided conversation.
SayBon Method
Exam-style practice Lessons are shaped around real DELF task types so learners understand what to expect.
Readiness focus The emphasis is on confidence, task familiarity, timing, and performance.
Progress tracking Learners can see where they are improving and what still needs attention.
Skill balance Preparation covers all four competencies, not just vocabulary or grammar.

Which Category?
DELF Prim Designed for children, usually ages 7–12. It uses child-friendly themes and age-appropriate communication.
DELF Junior Designed for teenagers, usually ages 12–17. It keeps the same exam seriousness with youth-appropriate contexts.
DELF Tout Public Designed for adults and general candidates. It focuses on practical, mature communication contexts.
Not sure? The recommendation test helps you choose the right path before committing to a preparation level.
